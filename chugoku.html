Chugoku

Introduction

The Chugoku region is rich in natural beauty, enjoyable walks, and historical significance. Hiroshima, with its well-known history and iconic floating shrine, is difficult to ignore, yet for those in the know, this popular location is only the gateway to western Japan. Continue west on the Sanyo Shinkansen to find picturesque beach villages and island-hopping options on the Seto Inland Sea, with connections to Kyushu and Shikoku. Travelling inland on the path less travelled will reward you with treks on beautiful peaks like Mt. Daisen, divine natural scenery, and the thrill of discovery in Shimane and Tottori.

Must-see Attractions

Interesting Facts
Puffer Fish Statue in Shimonoseki Scenery inside Akiyoshido
Shimonoseki, a city in the westernmost tip of Japan's largest island, Honshu, is a must-see for seafood enthusiasts. The city is well-known for its seafood, particularly pufferfish, which can be found at every fish market. Visit the stunning and adventurous Akiyoshido in Chugoku Region, the country's largest and longest limestone cave. Various natural formations may be seen along the trail, including terraces of water-filled limestone ponds, subterranean waterfalls, and a magnificent stream of cobalt-blue water flowing beside the path.


Samurai District in Hagi

Hagi is a tiny town in Yamaguchi prefecture, which is part of Japan's Chugoku region. It is well-known for its well-preserved samurai quarter, which consists of a maze of tiny lanes dotted with traditional Japanese-style buildings and dwellings. Hagi is also well-known for its Hagiyaki pottery, which has been made in Hagi for generations and is noted for its distinctive texture and colour. Visitors may observe potters at work in the town's workshops and stores, and even try their hand at manufacturing their own pottery. Hagi is a pleasant and intriguing site that gives visitors a taste of Japan's rich history and culture.



Summary

Chugoku, Japan's western portion of Honshu island, attracts travellers from all over the world with its unique combination of history, culture, and natural beauty. The region is also home to many UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the Hiroshima Peace Memorial and the Itsukushima Shrine, all of which give an insight into Japan's intriguing past. Chugoku is known for its spectacular natural beauty, which includes towering mountains, gorgeous coastline, and tranquil farmland, in addition to its cultural and historical attractions. Hiking routes, hot springs, and national parks, such as the Seto Inland Sea National Park, allow visitors to get up close and personal with Japan's beautiful environment.
