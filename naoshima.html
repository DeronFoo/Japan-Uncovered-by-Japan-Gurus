Naoshima

Introduction

Naoshima (直島) is a Japanese island in the Seto Inland Sea notable for its modern art museums, architecture, and sculptures. The island, which is part of Kagawa Prefecture, is a pleasant vacation from Japan's vast urban centres, with its Mediterranean ambience, sandy beaches, and sunny weather, paired with a laid-back, rural character.

The Benesse Corporation, which maintains art museums, installations, and sculptures on Naoshima and neighbouring islands, placed much of the island's art. The well-known Japanese architect Ando Tadao created Benesse's museums, which comprise the Chichu Art Museum, the Lee Ufan Museum, and all of the Benesse House's structures. Furthermore, Ishii Kazuhiro, a contemporary architect, designed Naoshima's municipal buildings and schools. The Setouchi Triennale art event takes place on the island.



Honmura

Honmura (本村) is a tiny port town on Naoshima's eastern coast that is home to the Art House Project, a collection of abandoned homes, a temple, and a shrine that have been turned into art installations and contemporary art venues by Japanese and international artists. The art homes are distributed across the city, and the majority of them appear unassuming from the street, fitting in with the surrounding traditional Japanese neighbourhood. The interiors of each, on the other hand, have been left to artists to transform into anything they see fit.



Benesse House

On Naoshima Island, the Benesse House is a modern art museum and vacation hotel. The complex is the focal point of the Benesse Corporation's art facilities on the island, and it comprises of four structures created by famous architect Ando Tadao: the Museum, Oval, Park, and Beach. Each structure has its own distinct artworks and guest rooms.

In addition to the art in the museum and the guest rooms, there are approximately twenty art works on the lawns and beaches around the Benesse House, including the iconic pumpkin (a reconstructed pumpkin is currently on exhibit after the original was damaged in a 2021 typhoon). Many of these may be located along the coastline and cliffs below the museum and have been specially constructed to blend in with their environment.



Summary

Overall, Naoshima is a must-see location for art enthusiasts and travellers looking for a one-of-a-kind cultural experience. It's the ideal destination to get away from the rush and bustle of city life, with its gorgeous art museums, stunning landscape, and peaceful environment. Visitors can reach Naoshima via ferry from numerous ports in the Seto Inland Sea area. Once on the island, shuttle buses and rental bicycles are available to help you travel about. Not to mention Naoshima has a wide range of lodging options, including hotels, guesthouses, and traditional ryokans. There are also a variety of dining alternatives for visitors, ranging from simple cafés to upmarket restaurants providing local seafood and other delicacies.
